Mutual Funds

A mutual fund is a financial instrument, which pools the money of different investors and then a fund manager invests them in different investment options and securities Investing in mutual fund is an easy way to diversify your investments across many securities and asset categories such as equity, debt and gold, which helps in spreading the risk - so you won't have all your eggs in one basket Most importantly, investing in mutual funds is very affordable. For those who cannot allocate a significant portion of their earnings towards mutual funds, they can start investing with amounts as low as Rs 100 at predefined intervals. This is known as a Systematic Investment Plan (SIP). On the contrary, if you have a significant chunk of money to invest, you can even make a lump sum investment in a mutual fund. Frequently Asked Questions

  • What is an Investment Advisory Firm?

    An investment advisory advises investors on buying and selling securities in exchange for a fee. An investment advisor can advise in two ways, offer advisory services directly to clients or issue investment advisory reports, videos, and other publications on specific securities. In a few cases, the investment advisory firm may use both options to offer advice.

  • Is financial advisory the same as wealth management?

    No, financial advisory and wealth management are not the same. The financial advisory provides investors with advice about buying and selling individual stocks. In contrast, wealth management is a broader term that includes a range of services such as portfolio management, estate planning, tax planning, and other financial services.

  • What Is The Role of an Investment / Stock Market Advisory Firm In India?

    A stock market advisory firm in India acts as an investment advisor, providing advice on stock market investing. Such investment advisors offer advice for a fee; however, they do not help you buy or sell equity and other securities. The decision to buy or sell in the Indian stock market lies with you.
